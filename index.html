Mission

“We understood the absurdity of the idea that nature and humanity can somehow be separated. We also recognized that the breakdown in our links to nature poses a genuine threat to humanity. Through the rapid urbanization of the past 100 years, we have lost touch with the wilderness, animals and plants that represent the very essence of life on Earth. We may know how to subjugate nature, but we easily forget that we depend on it for our very survival.”

Welcome

The images are all shot in stunning monochrome, with a breathtakingly beautiful range of tones on show that are reminiscent of the virtuoso work of Ansel Adams, and his way with light recalls the skills of the Old Master painters like Rembrandt. Curated by Lélia Wanick Salgado, Sebastião’s wife, the exhibition is divided into five geographic ‘chapters’ : Planet South, Sanctuaries, Africa, Northern Spaces, Amazonia and Pantanal. Salgado describes Genesis as ‘My love letter to the planet’ and I defy anyone seeing these immense works not to be touched by them, especially the works that feature isolated tribes from the Amazon, Papua New Guinea and the desert tribes of Africa. In them we see societies who’s lives show simultaneously a million degrees of separation from our modern western ones, whilst at the same time, showing us the essence (or quintessence) of homo sapien, begging the questions – have we progressed at all, what have we lost and how can we learn?
